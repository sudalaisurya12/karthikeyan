Contraception

What is it male contraception?

Men's birth control methods are referred to as male contraception. Condoms, withdrawal, vasectomy, and hormonal therapies are just a few of the alternatives.

Male Contraception Types

Condoms: Sperm cannot enter the vagina using this barrier technique. They can guard against sexually transmitted illnesses and are readily available.

Withdrawal: Alternatively referred to as the "pulling out" approach, this technique entails withholding the penis before to ejaculation to stop sperm from entering the vagina.

Vasectomy: This long-lasting male contraceptive procedure is surgically closing the tubes that transfer sperm from the testicles to the penis.

Hormonal Methods: These include using patches, gels, or injections that reduce sperm motility and production.

Reasons Men Get Pregnant Unintentionally

Unintended pregnancies among men can be caused by a variety of causes, such as inconsistent or improper use of birth control, access to contraception being restricted, and infertility or other medical issues.

Male Infertility: Diagnosis and Treatment A physical examination, a review of sexual and medical history, and laboratory tests are frequently used to diagnose male infertility. To help identify the underlying cause of infertility, procedures may include imaging examinations, hormone tests, and semen analyses.

Depending on the underlying cause, treatment options for male infertility may include drugs, surgery, or assisted reproductive methods like in vitro fertilisation (IVF).

Conclusion

To sum up, male contraception is a crucial component of family planning and can aid in avoiding unplanned pregnancies. Condoms, withdrawal, vasectomy, and hormonal therapies are just a few of the alternatives. To find the best approach for their particular circumstances, it is crucial for men to review their contraceptive alternatives with their healthcare professional.
